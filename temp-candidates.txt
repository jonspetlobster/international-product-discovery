South African Condiment Candidates - 2026-02-26

1. Mrs Balls Chutney (Peach)
- Sweet-tangy peach chutney, SA's #1 condiment
- Ubiquitous in SA, unknown in US
- Goes on everything: sandwiches, meat, cheese boards

2. Mrs Balls Chutney (Tomato)
- Spicy tomato chutney, breakfast staple
- Different from US ketchup - thicker, spicier, more complex

3. Chakalaka Relish
- Spicy vegetable relish with beans, peppers, tomatoes, curry spices
- Served with braai (BBQ), bread, pap
- No US equivalent

4. Monkey Gland Sauce
- Sweet-tangy BBQ sauce with fruit chutney base
- Iconic SA steakhouse sauce
- Name from 1920s medical procedure (not actual glands)

5. Boerewors Spice Blend
- Sausage seasoning: coriander seed, nutmeg, cloves, allspice
- Used to make traditional SA sausage
- Not available premixed in US

6. Cape Malay Curry Powder
- Unique curry blend from Cape Muslim community
- Different from Indian curry - sweeter, fruitier, milder
- Turmeric, coriander, cumin, fennel, ginger, cardamom

7. Peri-Peri Sauce (African Bird's Eye Chili)
- Though Nando's globalized it, authentic SA versions differ
- Made with African bird's eye chili (grown in SA)
- Most US versions use cayenne

8. Biltong Spice Mix
- Coriander seed, black pepper, vinegar cure for dried meat
- DIY biltong kit could work
- Different from beef jerky

9. Ina Paarman's Vegetable Seasoning
- All-purpose veggie spice blend beloved in SA
- Similar concept to Vegeta but SA flavor profile
- Not available in US

10. All Gold Tomato Sauce
- SA ketchup alternative, sweeter and thicker
- Bright red, iconic brand
- Different from Heinz

11. Wellington's Hot Chutney
- Spicy fruit chutney for meat
- SA brand, hard to find outside SA
- Sweet heat profile

12. Slap Chips Seasoning
- Unique SA french fry seasoning
- Aromat, vinegar, salt blend
- "Slap chips" = limp fries with vinegar

13. Steers BBQ Sauce
- Popular SA fast-food chain sauce
- Tangier, less sweet than US BBQ
- Cult following

14. Aromat Seasoning
- MSG-based all-purpose seasoning
- SA kitchen staple
- Banned/restricted in some US contexts

15. Rajah Curry Powder (SA Version)
- Specific SA formulation different from UK version
- Mild curry for traditional SA curries
- Yellow tin, iconic

16. Ina Paarman's BBQ Marinade
- Premium SA marinade brand
- Low-sugar, flavor-forward
- Popular for braai

17. Nola Mayonnaise (SA Recipe)
- Different from US mayo
- Tangier, more vinegar
- SA kitchen staple

18. Robertson's Spice Assortment
- SA spice brand with unique blends
- Steak & Chops spice is iconic
- Not available in US

19. Cape Malay Bobotie Spice
- Sweet curry meatloaf spice mix
- Includes dried fruit, curry, bay
- National dish seasoning

20. Simba Chutney Flavored Chips Seasoning
- Powder version of chutney chip flavor
- Sweet-tangy-spicy
- Could work as popcorn/veggie seasoning

21. Steers Monkey Gland Sauce (Bottled)
- Fast-food version of restaurant classic
- Sweet chutney-based BBQ
- Unique SA flavor

22. All Joy Tomato Relish
- Chunky tomato condiment
- Different from salsa, different from ketchup
- SA comfort food

23. Hellers Boerewors Spice Pack
- Ready-to-use sausage spice
- Just add to ground beef
- Makes SA traditional sausage

24. Cape Herb & Spice Rubs
- Premium SA spice company
- Rooibos-based rubs
- Unique ingredient (rooibos tea in savory blends)
